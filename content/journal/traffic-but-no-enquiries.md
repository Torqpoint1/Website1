Traffic without enquiries is one of the most common — and most solvable — problems a website has. It means the hard part is working: people are finding you. Something is going wrong between arriving and acting. Here’s the usual suspect list.

## The offer isn’t clear

If a visitor can’t tell what you do and who it’s for within seconds, they leave. Not because they weren’t interested — because they weren’t sure. Confusion is the quietest conversion killer there is.

## There’s no obvious next step

A site full of information but light on direction leaves people nodding and then closing the tab. Every page needs one clear, visible action — and it needs to repeat.

## Nothing’s building trust

- No reviews, so they can’t tell if you’re any good
- No real photos, so you feel faceless
- No answers to the obvious worries, so doubt wins

## The final step is too much effort

A long form, a hard-to-find phone number, a clunky mobile experience — any of these can lose someone who was ready. Make the last step feel effortless.

You’ve already paid for the visitors. Fixing the gap between visit and enquiry is often the highest-return work you can do — no extra traffic required.

There’s a category of lost business no analytics dashboard makes you feel: the people who clicked, waited, and gave up. They don’t leave a complaint. They just go to a competitor whose site loaded.

## Speed is trust you can’t see

A site that takes too long to appear feels broken, even when it isn’t. Patience online is thin, and it’s thinnest on mobile, where most local enquiries now begin.

## Mobile isn’t the smaller version — it’s the main one

If your site was designed on a big screen and “tested” there too, it may be quietly punishing the majority of your visitors. Buttons too small, text too cramped, forms that fight the thumb.

## What usually slows a site down

- Huge, unoptimised images straight off a phone
- Heavy templates loaded with features you don’t use
- Too many plugins and trackers piling up

Speed and mobile aren’t technical luxuries. They’re the difference between a lead arriving and a lead evaporating — and search engines reward the fast, mobile-friendly site over the slow one.

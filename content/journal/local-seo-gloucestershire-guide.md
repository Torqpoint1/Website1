When someone in Cheltenham, Gloucester, or Stroud types “[your service] near me,” Google has a few seconds to decide who to show. Local SEO is simply the work of being the obvious answer. Here’s where to spend your effort.

## Claim and complete your Google Business Profile

This is the single highest-return job for most local businesses. A complete profile — correct categories, service areas, photos, and steady reviews — is often what puts you on the map, literally.

## Make your location obvious on the site

Name the towns and areas you serve in your page copy, not just a postcode in the footer. If you cover the Cotswolds and the Forest of Dean, say so. Vague sites rank vaguely.

## Earn reviews on purpose

- Ask every happy client, the same day you finish
- Make it a one-tap link, not a chore
- Reply to each one — it signals you’re active and human

## Match the page to the search

Someone searching “bathroom fitter Gloucester” should land on a page about exactly that — not a generic homepage. The closer the page matches the question, the more likely it converts.

None of this is hype or trickery. It’s clarity, applied consistently. That’s what local search rewards.
